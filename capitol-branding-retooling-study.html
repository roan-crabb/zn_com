Reconnecting a National Wealth Team

A unified advisor platform and design system aligned Capitol Securities around one story, one toolkit, and one customer experience.

<- Back to Case Studies 

The challenge

Capitol Securities advisors work across 70+ offices with different tools, marketing assets, and onboarding rituals. Legacy intranet pages housed outdated resources, while the public site failed to reflect the firm’s research strength. Recruiting and client acquisition stalled because no one shared the same story.

I was brought in to align stakeholders—from compliance and marketing to IT—around a single experience vision that supported advisors in the field and amplified the firm’s credibility online.

What we did

  • Ran listening tours with advisors, compliance officers, and recruiting leaders to understand pain points and regulatory guardrails.
  • Mapped the end-to-end advisor journey, highlighting moments that required better tools, consistent messaging, or automated workflows.
  • Designed a modular design system spanning intranet, CRM, and public web properties, enabling teams to ship updates in weeks instead of months.
  • Built a content governance model so marketing, legal, and branch leaders could co-create insights without duplicating effort.

What we learned

Advisors didn’t need another portal—they needed the right information in the tools they already trusted. By integrating new components into existing workflows, we avoided the change-management drag that often derails enterprise transformations.

Equally important: compliance felt heard. Establishing shared language and review cadences meant the team could move faster without risking regulatory missteps.

Outcome

The new design system gave every advisor a consistent toolkit: branded pitch decks, automated proposal builders, and an insight library wired into the CRM. Prospective clients now see the same expertise advisors share in person, and recruits experience the firm’s culture before they sign.

The transformation also unlocked operational savings. Marketing reduced duplicated efforts, IT saw a 39% drop in ad-hoc requests, and leadership finally had the analytics they needed to steer growth.
