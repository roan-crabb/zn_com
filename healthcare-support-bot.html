Healthcare Support Bot Cuts Ticket Volume by 65%

A conversational assistant for a leading EHR now resolves HIPAA-sensitive issues in minutes and frees analysts to focus on complex care scenarios.

<- Back to Case Studies 

The challenge

Support queues ballooned as more hospitals adopted the client’s EHR platform. Analysts spent hours triaging redundant tickets, while clinicians waited days for answers to simple workflow issues. The knowledge base contained 70,000 articles, but it was impossible to navigate without knowing the exact jargon.

The company wanted to explore a generative AI assistant. My job was to design an experience that clinicians could trust, that legal could approve, and that analysts could maintain.

What we did

  • Mapped the full support ecosystem—from clinicians and practice admins to tier-three analysts—to understand intents, risk tolerance, and handoff rituals.
  • Audited the knowledge base, tagging HIPAA-sensitive content and surfacing contradictions that could confuse the model.
  • Designed a conversational UI with tone guidance, confidence indicators, and structured follow-ups when human escalation was required.
  • Built governance templates with legal and compliance covering prompt injection, PHI handling, and analyst override workflows.

What we learned

Trust hinged on transparency. Clinicians needed to know when they were interacting with AI, what data was being accessed, and how their conversation was stored. Analysts needed ways to monitor responses in real time and improve them without retraining a model.

By integrating the assistant into existing ticketing tools instead of forcing a separate portal, we respected established workflows and captured the analytics leaders needed to prove value.

Outcome

The AI assistant now resolves the majority of questions in seconds, and it guides clinicians to human help with context intact when needed. Analysts shifted from reactive triage to proactive knowledge management, improving content quality and closing gaps uncovered by the bot.

With success metrics in place, leadership expanded the program to cover onboarding, revenue-cycle operations, and patient portal support—each extension guided by the same responsible AI framework.
