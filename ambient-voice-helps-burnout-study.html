Humble PoC Helps Users and Defines an AI Future

A privacy-first pilot with 24 clinicians reclaimed documentation time and built executive confidence in ambient intelligence.

<- Back to Case Studies 

The challenge

Clinicians were exhausted by the promise of ambient voice tools. Previous attempts captured the wrong signals, heightened privacy fears, and created more work for providers already deep in burnout. The client needed proof that automation could feel safe, accurate, and genuinely helpful before investing in a multi-year roadmap.

I partnered with care teams, compliance, and engineering to define what “responsible innovation” looked like: short feedback loops, explicit consent checkpoints, and a pilot designed to learn—not to impress.

What we did

  • Embedded with providers across three specialties to map privacy concerns, task flows, and manual workarounds.
  • Co-created voice interaction scripts that mirrored real patient visits and surfaced sensitive terminology the model needed to respect.
  • Designed a “consent-first” capture app with transparent status cues so clinicians and patients understood exactly what was recorded.
  • Partnered with compliance to codify redlines and escalation triggers before any data left the device.

What we learned

The humble scope—just enough automation to remove repetitive note-taking—unlocked disproportionate trust. Clinicians cared less about flashy AI moments and more about reliable summaries they could edit quickly. The pilot also revealed that the biggest friction wasn’t the model, but the surrounding rituals: handoffs to medical scribes, audit requirements, and EHR shortcuts nobody documented.

Capturing those operational realities helped leadership reframe their roadmap. Instead of a monolithic release, we recommended a series of progressive enhancements tied to clear adoption metrics and ongoing consent conversations.

Outcome

The pilot gave executives tangible proof that ambient intelligence could reduce burnout without compromising privacy. Clinical leadership committed to scaling the approach, and the organization funded an AI governance council grounded in the playbook we created.

Most importantly, clinicians became co-authors of the roadmap. Their feedback prioritized 12 experiments across specialties—from auto-generating visit summaries to helping nurses prep orders. Adoption targets, not hype cycles, now drive the investment conversation.
