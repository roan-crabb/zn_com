The Power of Presence: How We Helped Burnt Out Physicians Reclaim Precious Pajama Time, Increase Patient Facetime, & Provide Better Overall Care

The Challenge: When Technology Becomes a Barrier

Here's a sobering reality about modern healthcare: physicians spend nearly half their day looking at screens instead of patients. For one leading EHR (Electronic Health Record) company, this wasn't just a usability issue – it was a crisis of connection threatening the very heart of patient care.

Their physicians were facing a painful double-bind: either maintain eye contact with patients and work late into the evening completing notes (the dreaded "pajama time"), or type furiously during appointments while trying to maintain some semblance of connection. Neither option was sustainable, and both were taking a toll on physician wellbeing and patient satisfaction.

Our initial research revealed a healthcare system struggling under the weight of its own documentation requirements. The numbers painted a stark picture: physicians were spending up to 6 hours daily on EHR documentation, with an average of 2.5 hours of "pajama time" per physician per day. Less than 50% of appointment time was spent making eye contact with patients, leading to rising physician burnout rates and decreased patient satisfaction.

Even more telling were the hidden costs. Medical errors increased by 30% during the final hours of long documentation sessions. Patient satisfaction scores showed a direct correlation with physician screen time – the more a doctor typed during appointments, the lower their satisfaction ratings dropped. Meanwhile, physician burnout rates had reached an alarming 63% among those reporting more than 2 hours of daily after-hours documentation.

Our Approach: Understanding the Full Clinical Journey

Rather than jumping straight to solutions, we started with extensive discovery research to understand the complete physician workflow. Our comprehensive research included:

  • 45+ hours of clinical shadowing across different specialties
  • In-depth interviews with 30 physicians
  • Analysis of over 100 daily schedule patterns
  • Documentation time studies across multiple practice types

This thorough investigation revealed critical insights about workflow breakpoints and context switching costs. Perhaps most importantly, it helped us understand the emotional toll of constant documentation – something that wouldn't show up in simple time-motion studies.

In addition to extensive clinical research, we made sure to also pay attention to the other details that often go overlooked. Designing workflows that take full advantage of the device's hardware – like biometrics to make logging in a breeze. As well as carefully crafted branding to help bolster the ideal user emotions and app "feel".

Journey Mapping: Uncovering the Real Story

Our research culminated in a comprehensive journey map that revealed patterns we hadn't anticipated. Yes, physicians were spending up to 75% of each appointment focusing on the EHR instead of the patient, and "pajama time" had become the norm. But the journey map showed something even more interesting: there were natural conversation patterns in patient visits that could be leveraged for documentation. We identified five key moments in every patient visit where documentation needs peaked:

Patient History Updates

Current Symptoms Discussion

Physical Examination Findings

Treatment Plan Development

Orders & Follow-up Instructions

These moments became our focal points for design intervention. We also discovered that physicians had developed their own workarounds – from using text expansion tools to hiring scribes – suggesting a deep need for a more systematic solution.

Design Evolution: From Concept to Reality

Creating an ambient voice solution that physicians would actually trust wasn't just about technical capabilities – it was about understanding the subtle rhythms of patient interactions. Our design went through three major iterations:

Version 1: The Baseline

Our first prototype focused on simple voice recording with basic transcription. While functional and helpful, physicians found it still could be greatly improved to further streamline the process.

Version 2: Context Awareness

We added intelligent context recognition that could differentiate between casual conversation and clinical documentation needs. This version started to show real promise, reducing review & editing time by 40%.

Version 3: The Breakthrough

The final design incorporated predictive section mapping and real-time structure analysis. By understanding the natural flow of patient visits, the system could automatically organize information into the right sections of a SOAP note, even when conversations didn't follow a linear pattern.

The Solution: Ambient Intelligence

Working in partnership with Nabla, we designed an ambient voice solution that would fundamentally transform the documentation process. The system includes:

  • One-touch recording activation integrated with daily schedule
  • Automatic SOAP note generation from natural conversation
  • Smart diagnosis coding and direct EHR integration
  • Privacy-first design with clear recording indicators
  • Real-time quality checks and verification

We built intelligence into every aspect of the workflow, from context-aware appointment tracking to automated section parsing and organization. Physicians can use quick-edit tools for real-time modifications and flexible review workflows, ensuring accuracy without disrupting patient interactions.

The voice assistant nature of the application helps providers get back to doing what they do best – connecting & caring for their patients. For decades, these providers have slogged through clunky, inefficient workflows for hours per day. There's no telling how much time & money has been spent on endless, frustrating hours behind a keyboard.

Continuous Evolution: Beyond the Initial Launch and Into the Future

The success of this project has made it the EHR company's leading product offering, but the story doesn't end there. Post-launch monitoring and feedback revealed new opportunities for improvement, launching our AI Enhancement Program that makes the system smarter with every use. It's now learning from physician corrections, adapting to individual documentation styles, improving specialty-specific terminology recognition, and enhancing context awareness for complex cases.

This project has shown a path forward for healthcare technology that enhances rather than inhibits human connection. The solution continues to evolve, with several exciting developments on the horizon:

  • Multi-language support for diverse patient populations and global healthcare settings
  • Integration with medical imaging for comprehensive documentation and analysis
  • Real-time clinical decision support enhancing diagnostic accuracy and treatment planning
  • Enhanced mobile capabilities enabling seamless remote and bedside care

Perhaps the most meaningful outcome isn't captured in metrics or features. We've seen a cultural shift in how physicians view technology – from a burden to be endured to a partner in delivering care. As one physician put it, "For the first time in years, I feel like I can really be present with my patients again."

Technology should enhance, not replace, human connection. Understanding the full user journey is crucial for transformative solutions, but success goes beyond efficiency to include quality of life. When we design with human connection in mind, we can transform not just workflows, but lives. Remember: In healthcare technology, the goal isn't just to make processes more efficient – it's to create space for the human connections that make medicine meaningful.
